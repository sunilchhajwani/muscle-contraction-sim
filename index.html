References & Physiology

Sliding filament theory — Huxley & Hanson (1954). Actin filaments slide toward M-line via myosin head power strokes. Sarcomere shortens 2.2→1.6 µm.

Cross-bridge cycle — 5 steps: ① Attach (myosin binds actin) ② Power stroke (Pi release, head pivots ~45°) ③ ADP release ④ ATP binding → detach ⑤ ATP hydrolysis → recock head.

Ca²⁺ dynamics — AP triggers SR release (~10ms), [Ca²⁺] rises from 0.1→10 µmol/L. SERCA pump reuptake (~100ms). Troponin C binds Ca²⁺ → tropomyosin shifts → sites exposed.

Rigor — ATP depleted: heads remain attached (post-power-stroke, no ATP to detach). Muscle stiff.

References: Guyton & Hall Textbook of Medical Physiology, 14th ed. Ch. 6-7.
